Quick Topic Review

Section 2: Fundamentals of Security
  • CIA Triad: confidentiality prevents unauthorized disclosure, integrity prevents unauthorized change, availability keeps systems reachable.
  • Non-repudiation uses digital signatures: hash the message, encrypt the hash with the sender's private key, verify with the sender's public key.
  • AAA order matters: authenticate first, authorize second, account/log after access.
  • Zero Trust: never trust, always verify. Policy Engine decides in the control plane; PEP executes in the data plane.
  • Risk exists only when a threat and a matching vulnerability are both present.
Section 3: Threat Actors
  • Threat actor is the who, threat vector is the how, attack surface is the where.
  • Nation-state/APT usually signals high sophistication, long-term persistence, custom tooling, espionage, or strategic goals.
  • Organized crime is money-driven, even when tooling is advanced or the group acts as a proxy.
  • Shadow IT is usually convenience-driven, not automatically malicious, but still creates unmanaged risk.
  • Honey family by scale: honeytoken, honeyfile, honeypot, honeynet.
